The life of Mac Miller

Early Life

Mac Miller, real name Malcolm James McCormick, was always destined for music. Even young in life he was always ryhming and showing his talent. Life had not always been easy as he struggled in schooling, eventually deciding to end his schooling early. However, nothing was going to hold him back from his true passion.

Jumping on the Scene

Mac Miller first came onto the scene under the alias "Easy Mac", coming out with his first self released mixtape 'But My Mackin' Ain't Easy'. A few years later he would begin releasing music under Mac Miller and released some of his more popular mixtapes like 'K.I.D.S', 'Best Day Ever', and 'Macadelic'.


Mac's first studio album title 'Blue Slide Park' garnered enough attention to land him on the Billboard 200, and his achievements would only rise from there. He would go on to release several notable albums like 'GO:OD AM', 'The Divine Feminine', and his last album before his untimely end 'Swimming'.


After his unfortunate death at the age of on 26 years old, Mac left one last gift for his fans with his posthumous album 'Circles'. The unknowning second part of his 'Swimming' album. Mac left his mark on his fans and the music industry which will last forever.

Legacy

For all Mac's fans, he will forever be a part of their lives. Even outside of the music Mac only spread messages of positivity, love, and passion. Truly, he was one-of-a-kind and an inspiration.
